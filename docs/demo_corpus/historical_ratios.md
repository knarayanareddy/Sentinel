### Commentary

The Debt/EBITDA ratio has been on an upward trajectory since Q3 FY2023, driven by two factors: (1) a $280M increase in consolidated debt associated with the Meridian acquisition in Q4 FY2023, and (2) a gradual decline in trailing EBITDA from the $405M peak in Q4 FY2023.

**Q4 FY2024:** The ratio approached the 4.50x threshold for the first time, reaching 4.51x. Management issued an internal cautionary memo and initiated a review of the capital structure. No covenant breach was triggered as the ratio was marginally above threshold; the Treasury team attributed the 0.01x overage to timing differences in accounts receivable.

**Q1 FY2025:** The ratio moved above the Maximum Leverage Ratio of 4.50x, reaching 4.62x. This triggered the 30-day cure period under §4.2 of the Credit Agreement. Management attributes the deterioration to a $40M EBITDA decline driven by three discrete transaction-related costs in the recent 60-day period (see Transaction Analysis Report, dated June 20, 2025).

**Q2 FY2025:** The ratio remains elevated at 4.74x. The cure period from Q1 has expired, and an Event of Default notification is currently under review by the Administrative Agent's counsel.

### Trend Analysis

The consistent upward movement in the ratio over eight consecutive quarters indicates a structural issue rather than a one-time fluctuation. The EBITDA decline from $405M (Q4 FY2023) to $380M (Q2 FY2025) represents a 6.2% deterioration, while debt has increased by 18.4% over the same period.

### Recommended Actions

1. **Immediate:** Prepare escalation memo to Administrative Agent per §4.2 cure provisions
2. **Short-term:** Evaluate voluntary prepayment of $50M in subordinated notes to reduce the ratio below 4.50x
3. **Medium-term:** Commission a full operational review to identify EBITDA improvement opportunities
4. **Long-term:** Engage financial advisors to evaluate refinancing options
