## Executive Summary

This report details material transactions occurring during the 60-day period from April 15 to June 14, 2025, that impacted Consolidated EBITDA and contributed to the Debt/EBITDA covenant breach reported in Q1 FY2025. Three anomalous transactions are identified below that, in aggregate, reduced reported EBITDA by approximately $27.5M.

## Anomalous Transaction Detail

### 🔴 Transaction #1 — April 18: Meridian Q2 Management Fee Accrual ($8.2M)

The Meridian subsidiary recorded a Q2 management fee accrual of $8.2M, representing a 63% increase over the prior quarter's accrual of $5.0M. This accrual was based on a revised fee structure adopted by Meridian's board on April 1, 2025. The fee increase was not communicated to the parent company's Treasury department until after the accrual was booked. Investigation revealed the fee increase was retroactive to January 1, 2025, but was booked entirely in Q2 rather than spread across quarters. This single transaction accounts for $3.2M of the total $27.5M EBITDA impact.

### 🔴 Transaction #5 — May 8: Meridian Restructuring Accrual ($11.8M)

Meridian recorded an $11.8M restructuring accrual associated with the planned consolidation of its European operations. This accrual covers estimated severance costs, facility closure expenses, and asset write-downs. The restructuring plan was not approved by Meridian's board until May 15, 2025 — seven days after the accrual was recorded. The premature booking of this accrual, combined with its size, represents the single largest EBITDA impact in the 60-day window. Treasury has requested that Meridian's CFO provide documentation supporting the timing and amount of this accrual.

### 🔴 Transaction #8 — May 28: Meridian Legal Settlement Reserve ($7.5M)

Meridian established a $7.5M legal settlement reserve in connection with a pending intellectual property dispute filed by a former executive. The reserve was booked in full despite the litigation being in early discovery stages, with no settlement demand yet received from the plaintiff. Legal counsel has advised that the probability of settlement is "reasonably possible" but not "probable" under ASC 450, which would typically preclude full reserve recognition at this stage. This reserve booking reduced EBITDA by $7.5M and contributed to the covenant breach.

## Recommendations

1. **Engage Meridian CFO** to review accrual timing policies and ensure compliance with ASC 450 and GAAP requirements
2. **Request supporting documentation** for the $11.8M restructuring accrual — book date precedes board approval
3. **Evaluate legal counsel's assessment** of the IP dispute reserve — full recognition at early discovery stage is unusual
4. **Consider restatement discussion** with Administrative Agent if accruals are found to be improperly timed
5. **Escalate to Audit Committee** if accrual practices suggest potential earnings management
